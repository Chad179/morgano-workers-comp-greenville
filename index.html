When does a South Carolina business need workers compensation insurance?

Workers compensation should be reviewed before hiring, taking on subcontractors, bidding jobs, or signing contracts. Requirements depend on the business and should be checked with current South Carolina rules.

Short answer: A practical workers compensation review looks at employee count, payroll, class codes, excluded owners, subcontractor certificates, contract requirements, and audit records.

What employee and payroll details affect workers compensation?

Payroll, job duties, class codes, ownership, and state-specific rules all affect workers compensation pricing and eligibility.

Why do subcontractor certificates matter in South Carolina?

Uninsured subcontractors can create audit and liability issues. Keep current certificates and review how each carrier treats subcontractor exposure.

How do class codes affect a workers comp quote?

Class codes group workers by the type of work performed. A clerical employee and a roofer do not carry the same risk, so accurate classification matters.

What records help during a workers compensation audit?

Payroll reports, owner/officer details, subcontractor certificates, job descriptions, and prior policy documents help keep audits cleaner.

Common follow-up questions about this Greenville insurance topic

Is workers comp the same as general liability?

No. Workers compensation addresses employee work injuries. General liability addresses certain third-party claims.

Should a new hire trigger a coverage review?

Yes. Hiring can change whether coverage is needed and how payroll should be reported.
